Using Census and Deaf School Records to Find Deaf Ancestors and Family Members

This page is intended to be a record of research techniques and links to finding relatives who may have appeared in various nineteeth- and twentieth-century United States Federal Census and state residential Deaf school (historically called ‘asylums’ and ‘institutions‘) records.

(This presentation is mostly U.S.-centric, but research principles can be applied to other countries with school records.) American Deaf school records are very effective in triangulating/confirming and adding sources. They can help re-create stories instead of just gathering additional facts.

Thanks to all of the staff and participants at the 2018 Deaf Family History Workshop.
