How does this class fit into the rest of the interpreting classes here at UVU?

ASL3350 is named Consecutive Interpreting, focusing on an introduction to bidirectional (ASL-to-English and English-to-ASL) consecutive interpreting theory and practice between Deaf and nondeaf people. (If you were enrolled at UVSC (Utah Valley State College) prior to the Fall 03 semester, ASL3350 was previously titled ASL4320.) Here’s where it currently fits into the program (this list is not comprehensive):

In addition, since 2006, UVU has offered an independently-funded Advanced Certification Interpreter Preparation Program (ACIPP) which focuses on currently-certified interpreters looking for an academic track to help them obtain advanced state or national interpreter certification. If you’re interested in the ACIPP, you can learn more about it here.

How do I get a good grade in this class?

Course final grades are determined by combining both (1) scores on all of the submitted assignments and exams and (2) considering assessed performance on your student-teacher contract. More importantly, you should remember that making decisions in interpreting situations or producing interpreting work is really all about efficient and fluent problem solving: identifying and then applying knowledge to resolve communication differences.

While some portions of your coursework and quizzes will test for specific factual information (remembering skills), far less important is your ability to regurgitate facts and figures. Instead, assignments, labs, and the final exam are designed to identify how well you internalize principles, cultural and technical knowledge, and process understanding, and then apply them to create solutions to various situations (applying and analyzing skills).
