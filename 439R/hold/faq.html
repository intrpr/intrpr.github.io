How does this class fit into the rest of the interpreting classes here at UVU?

This section of ASL439R is named Resources for Professional Sign Language Interpreters, a collaborative seminar designed to 1) bring students and the professional signed language interpreter community together to 2) create an up-to-date, online open-source resource of professional practices, standards, and support materials. Here’s where it currently fits into the program (this list is not comprehensive):

Course Availability
ASL3310: Introduction to Interpreting Fall
ASL3320: Physiology of Interpreting Spring, Summer, Fall
ASL3330: Cross-Cultural Interpreting Spring
ASL3340: Interpreting II—Advanced Techniques Fall
ASL3350: Consecutive Interpreting on demand
ASL3360: Simultaneous Interpreting on demand
ASL3370: Sign to Voice Interpreting on demand
ASL3380: Transliteration on demand
ASL3390: Professional Issues in Interpreting on demand
ASL4330: Visual Linguistic Analysis on demand
ASL4360: Legal Interpreting on demand
ASL370: Ethics for Interpreters on demand
ASL439R: Special Topics in Interpreting on demand

As of the end of Fall Semester 2010, the ACIPP program has been discontinued. Please contact the Foreign Language office or Dr. Eldredge for more information about future UVU interpreting paracertificate programs. UVU does offer, however, an independently-funded Advanced Certification Interpreter Preparation Program (ACIPP) to interpreters looking for a highly-focused academic track to help them obtain advanced state or national interpreter certification. Read more about it at their website.

Read ASL class descriptions from the College Web site or if you’re interested in learning how these courses are applied towards your degree, contact ASL & DS Coordinator, Dr. Bryan Eldredge (bryane (at) uvu.edu), LA003f; (801) 863-8529 V/VP.

How do I get a good grade in this class?

Course final grades are determined by combining both

  1. participation in all class discussions and workgroup activities, and maintenance of reading schedules. Some of our coursework will only be completed in class or within group work; your involvement in coursework is necessary to the success of our efforts
  2. grades given by your peers
  3. performance on your student-teacher contract.

Your efforts in maintaining the currency of our efforts will wholly rest on your research, maintenance of the reading schedule, and participation in the class. This is not a typical show-up-and-be-talked-at course: we will explore topics and materials together, often requiring you to take the lead in researching a particular topic and/or becoming the class expert on a given subject or topic. The collaborative nature of this course allows us all to learn from one another while producing an important resource for our professional community. Please come prepared to search for and talk about answers, not expect the instructor to impart wisdom in an Powerpoint presentation.

  • Want to learn more about higher-level learning? Try Bloom’s (revised) Taxonomy or this list of action verbs based on Bloom’s. Skills higher up on the pyramid are necessary for equivalent interpreting; our class assignments and exams will require you to demonstrate performance on several levels. (Anderson, L. W., & Krathwohl, D. R. (Eds.). (2001). A taxonomy for learning, teaching and assessing: A revision of Bloom’s Taxonomy of educational objectives. New York: Longman.)

Scoring criteria are clearly defined on project completion and peer review; you’ll know what you need to do to earn the grade you want. Ultimately, your grade is a reflection of personal effort and internalization of concepts learned in the course.

When are assignments due? When is the final? What’s on the quizzes? The exams?

All schedule-related questions can be answered on the class schedule. Final exams cannot be given early or on any other date than what is proscribed by the college. Please don’t ask. The final “exam” will be a group presentation of your semester efforts, research, and contribution to the materials. Read more on the assignments page.

Do you have any class notes or handouts on this site?

The information you/your group researches and produces will be available at any time in our agreed-upon collaboration tool. Sometimes, specific class notes or charts may be distributed on an infrequent basis. All of the class handouts, materials, and research areas can be found on the schedule.
