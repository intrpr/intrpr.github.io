Signs for Middle Eastern, North African, and Arabic countries

Arabic Sign Language (لغة لإشارة)

Please note: this site requires additional communication with YouTube; extra loading time may be required.

Shown below are Arabian SL-based signs for various Middle Eastern, Northern African, and predominently Arabic countries; at the bottom are additional signs for non-Middle Eastern/African but predominantly Muslim countries. It has been discussed that SL of Jordan, Lebanon, Syria, Palestine, Iraq, and possibly Saudi Arabia have a great deal of overlap and cognate similarity; that stated, as there is no universal signed language, these signs may or may not represent universal usage throughout these geographic areas. This project/site has been created to 1) study overlap between Arabic SL and other Western (e.g., ASL, CASL, BANZSL) country/geographical sign representations and 2) to increase interpreter fluency (but not necessarily usage) with these lexical items.

Update: Dan Parvaz points out that these signs “look by and large like their indigenous country signs.” However, he also corrects that this Arabic SL lexicon is the product of non-Deaf peoples attemping to homogenize the SLs of this area of the world; there are several variations on these signs that continue to be used by the indigenous Deaf peoples of these countries. Additionally, Dan explains that neighboring countries tend to share more commonalities in geographic signs, “say Jordan and Syria, have far more in common than distant ones, say Jordan and Tunisia.”
