Topics and Readings

Topic / Date Assignments / Exams Readings

What is interpreting?

  
Chapter 1 (pp. 1–10)
Read pp. 1-4.
(This copy is only for students who have not received their books; this copy is not for distribution or resale.) 576K
Entry to Practice Competencies for ASL/English Interpreters (DO-IT Center)
Read pp. 1-8.
This white paper outlines five domains—theory and knowledge, human relations, language skills, interpreting skills, and professionalism competencies—that professional interpreters are expected to demonstrate in their work environments.
Is There a Psychology of the Hearing?
This article by Hoffmeister & Harvey investigates motivations behind why non-deaf people learn sign language and/or work with Deaf people, how hearing people perceive Deaf people and American Sign Language. Compare with Cokely (2001) Creating Rich Realities. Contrast this reading with Stewart, Schein, & Cartrwight’s similar proposition on pp. 1–4 of Chapter 1. 2.9MB

Historical evidences of interpreting
Jan 23
Chapter 2 Outline
Chapter 2 (pp. 13–27)
Handout (Stringham)
A timeline of interpreting history and dates that affect the necessity of sign language interpreters. 52K
Handout (Giambruno)
Giambruno writes about Title 29, Book II of Leyes de las Indias, political documents and statutes which governed the employment of interpreters by Spanish colonies in the New World. 7.5MB
About the Rosetta Stone
This video (and link to several others) provided by the British Museum gives an explanation of the Rosetta Stone, discovered in 1799 by Napoleon and Champollion. Video in BSL.
http://delicious.com/dsasl/asl3310ch02

Cognitive and interactive models of interpreting
Jan 30
Chapter 3 Outline
Chapter 3 (pp. 29–44)
Handout (Schein, Stewart & Cartwright/Stringham)
A basic interactive and cognitive model of interpreting. Pages 2–6 show how the model can be broken down and analyzed by participants, message, environments, and cognitive processes. 560K
Handout (Cokely)
Cokely’s Sociolinguistic Model of Interpretation. Considered the benchmark of scientific and analytic intepreting models, Cokely’s diagram measures interpreting miscues in a scientific manner and helps interpreters determine where they happen. 20K
Handout (Colonomos)
Colonomos’ CRP Pedagogical Model of Interpretation. Colonomos’ work focuses on ascertaining the meaning and intent behind a speaker’s message. This model helps interpreters determine where, in the process, they should be attending to specific skills and efforts. 300K
Handout (McIntire & Sanderson)
A comparison of four historical approaches (McIntire & Sanderson, 1993) to interpreting situations. 24K
http://delicious.com/dsasl/asl3310ch03

Application of Demand-Control Theory to Sign Language Interpreting: Implications for Stress and Interpreter Training (Dean & Pollard)
This seminal work in the DC-S theory framework introduces various types of demands—linguistic, environmental, interpersonal, and intrapersonal—and discusses their impacts on interpreter environments and psychology.
Handout (RID)
RID’s official position paper on Cumulative Motion Injuries: information about, types of, and prevention of various injuries.
Preventative Exercises
A variety of preventative exercises that interpreters (or anyone with potential overuse syndrome symptoms) can use.

Language and culture
Feb 29
Chapter 7 Outline
Chapter 7 (pp. 105–118)
Handout (Stringham)
A comparative language continuum demonstrating the overlapping relationship of American Sign Language and English which results in manually coded English (MCE) forms and approaches (PSE, CASE, SSS, SimCom, TC). Identifies and briefly describes the most commonly known and/or used languages and MCE forms and approaches. 204K
Handout (Gustason, 1990)
An article by Gustason (in Bornstein, 1990) examines the origins of the SEE and LOVE MCEs. Particularly helpful in clarifying LOVE and reasons for and myths about SEE creation and usage. 1.4MB
Handout (Bornstein, et al 1983)
The preface to The Comprehensive Signed English Dictionary by Bornstein (1983) explains some of the methodology behind Bornstein and Saulnier’s MCE system. Describes SE’s affixes and rationale behind their sign creation rules. 883K
Origins, Formations, Rules, and (Mis)Usage of ASL Name Signs (Stringham)
http://delicious.com/dsasl/asl3310ch07

The business of interpreting
Mar 19
Chapter 8 Outline
Chapter 8 (pp. 121–139)
Networking for the Developing Sign Language Interpreter
This posting at the interpreting blog Street Leverage discusses the why and the how for getting off the ground as a new interpreter/interpreter candidate. Excellent resource. Correlate this with our in-class guest speaker on this topic.
Center for Disease Control (CDC) Report on the US Deaf Community (March 2011)
From the abstract, this report discusses pathological (not “hearing/auditory”) issues confronting Deaf people, “we describe 4 issues that underlie health inequities experienced by deaf sign language users and propose 6 public health approaches to address these health and health care inequities to promote health and prevent chronic diseases.” Includes a two-part ASL video which discusses the concept of social justice.
Market Disorder Within the Field of Sign Language Interpreting: Professionalization Implications (Witter-Merithew & Johnson, 2004)
This reprint of their 2004 Journal of Interpretation article discusses market disorder and areas of weakness and threat in the professional sign language interpreter space.
2011 Annual Report to the Members of the RID (RID, 2011)
Despite the recounting of their 2010–2011 FY, the RID’s annual report does give some statistics about who took exams, who passed/failed (p. 10–11) and where the membership lives (p. 12–14).
NIC Testing Preparation
It’s the How, Not the What: Preparing for the NIC Interview
State of Utah QA Testing Preparation
Utah Interpreter Program Certification Requirements (June 2009)
This is the newest revised list of requirements for the State of Utah’s Certification/QA program. Find exact policies, downloads for testing criteria, schedules, applications, and a practice exam.
State of Utah Interpreter and Transliterator Policy and Procedures Manual (2005)
This manual prepared by the Utah State Department of Rehabilitation outlines policies, procedures, and state legal statutes outlining the provision of interpreters in the state of Utah. UIP recommends you read this for Written Exam preparation. 172K
Written Exam Preparation Handout (2005)
This is a handout outlining preparation, expectations, and recommended reading in preparation for taking the Utah State Written Exam. 36K
Practice Written Exam (2005)
Now retired by UIP, this is a Practice written exam administered by the Utah Interpreting Program from 2000–2009; for a practice test based on the June 2009 revision, see the link above. 20K
Utah Code 53A-26a: Interpreter Services for the Hearing Impaired Act
Utah Code 78B-1-201: Definitions (of an interpreter/transliterator)
Utah Code 78B-1-202: Proceedings at which interpreter is to be provided for hearing impaired.
American Sign Language Interpreters in Utah State Courts

The ethics of interpreting
Mar 26
Bibliography 2
Mar 28
Chapter 9 Outline
Chapter 9 (pp. 143–162), RID CPC
RID Code of Professional Conduct (2005)
Webpage itemizing the Code of Professional Conduct of the Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf, approved 2005. This is not found in your text.
Handout (Ethics 2005)
The recently updated Code of Professional Conduct of the Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf, approved 2005. This is not found in your text. 76K
Handout (Ethics 1979)
The previous (and most well known) compilation of the Code of Ethics of the Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf. This list is also found in your text. 300K
“Ethical Development: A Sign of the Times for Sign Language Interpreters?” (Dean)
Dean (re)presents the case for supervision in the professional interpreting field. First proposed in 1986 (Fritsch-Rudser, S. (1986). The RID code of ethics, confidentiality and supervision. Journal of Interpretation, 3, 47-51.), supervision is the next step the field needs to take regarding ethical behavior and interpreter health and well-being. Must read.
“Case Conferencing and Supervision for Interpreters” (Dean & Pollard, 2009)
Discusses the concept of case presentation, superision, and interpreting as a practice profession. From the RID VIEWS, Fall 2009.
The Impact of Social Media Interactions on Interpreter Ethics (Butler)
Exploring Ethics: A Case for Revising the Code of Ethics (Cokely, 2000)
Discusses an approach to revising the (then) RID Code of Ethics, changing from a deontological (presecriptive) to teleological (rights/purpose-based) approach.
Satisfaction and Negative Experiences of Deaf Leaders with Interpreters (Forestal)
Professional Behaviors & Dispositions Self Evaluation Form
The purpose of this self-evaluation is to reflect on your own professional behavior and dispositions. Answer questions as you think your peers may perceive you, not as you wish your answers to be. This is not found in your text. 390K
http://delicious.com/dsasl/asl3310ch09
